Raised in Phoenix, I first delved into film my sophomore year along with Youth and Government.


I became passionate about sharing my views, and I learned to express myself through my content. My passion developed with every film video created with. That passion then transformed into making videos for non-profits. My first advertisement video was one made with a friend for a charter school called Vista College Preparatory in an impoverished part of Phoenix. Afterwards, I created a video for a YMCA award ceremony. The project led to me being able to attain a job at the YMCA as a videographer and cinematographer.


My passion for film and political advocacy continues to drive me forward. I not only want to make films, but I want to make films that matter. That is why I have worked with nonprofits and hope to make more creative films that have an impact.
